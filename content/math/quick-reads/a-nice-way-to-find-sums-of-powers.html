A Nice Way to Find Sums of Powers

The specific example

Let's start with a specific example, later we will generalise this method.

Solve the following sum:

∑k=0k∈Nn2k\sum_{\substack{k=0 \\ k \in \mathbb{N}}}{n}2kk=0k∈N​∑n​2k

We can start by doing this expansion:

∑k=0n2k=∑k=0n1⋅2k=1⋅2n+1⋅2n−1+⋯+1⋅21+1⋅20\sum_{k=0}{n}2k = \sum_{k=0}{n}1 \cdot 2k = 1 \cdot 2n + 1 \cdot 2{n-1} + \cdots + 1 \cdot 21 + 1 \cdot 20k=0∑n​2k=k=0∑n​1⋅2k=1⋅2n+1⋅2n−1+⋯+1⋅21+1⋅20

Now we can observe that this forms a number in binary system:

1⋅2n+1⋅2n−1+⋯+1⋅21+1⋅20=111…111⏟n+1(2)∑k=0n2k=111…111⏟n+1(2)\begin{gather*} 1 \cdot 2n + 1 \cdot 2{n-1} + \cdots + 1 \cdot 21 + 1 \cdot 20 = \underbrace{111 \ldots 111}_{n+1}{}_{(2)} \\ \sum_{k=0}{n}2k = \underbrace{111 \ldots 111}_{n+1}{}_{(2)} \end{gather*}1⋅2n+1⋅2n−1+⋯+1⋅21+1⋅20=n+1111…111​​(2)​k=0∑n​2k=n+1111…111​​(2)​​

We can now add 111 to both sides of equation which gives:

1+∑k=0n2k=1000…000⏟n+1(2)1 + \sum_{k=0}{n}2k = 1\underbrace{000 \ldots 000}_{n+1}{}_{(2)}1+k=0∑n​2k=1n+1000…000​​(2)​

Converting the binary number to its decimal form:

1+∑k=0n2k=2n+1  ⟹  ∑k=0n2k=2n+1−11 + \sum_{k=0}{n}2k = 2^{n+1} \implies \sum_{k=0}{n}2k = 2^{n+1} - 11+k=0∑n​2k=2n+1⟹k=0∑n​2k=2n+1−1

Generalising

Now lets change our sum to base ppp where p∈Np \in \mathbb{N}p∈N

∑k=0k∈Nnpk\sum_{\substack{k=0 \\ k \in \mathbb{N}}}{n}pkk=0k∈N​∑n​pk

The start is identical, but now we multiply by p−1p - 1p−1 instead of 111.

(p−1)∑k=0npk=(p−1)⋅pn+(p−1)⋅pn−1+⋯+(p−1)⋅p1+(p−1)⋅p0(p - 1)\sum_{k=0}{n}pk = (p - 1) \cdot pn + (p - 1) \cdot p{n-1} + \cdots + (p - 1) \cdot p1 + (p - 1) \cdot p0(p−1)k=0∑n​pk=(p−1)⋅pn+(p−1)⋅pn−1+⋯+(p−1)⋅p1+(p−1)⋅p0

This sequence can be substituted for a number in base ppp.

(p−1)∑k=0npk=ζζζ…ζζζ⏟n+1(p)(p - 1)\sum_{k=0}{n}pk = \underbrace{\zeta \zeta \zeta \ldots \zeta \zeta \zeta}_{n+1}{}_{(p)}(p−1)k=0∑n​pk=n+1ζζζ…ζζζ​​(p)​

where ζ\zetaζ is the biggest digit in base ppp that is equal to p−1p - 1p−1.

Now again adding 111

1+(p−1)∑k=0npk=1000…000⏟n+1(p)1 + (p - 1)\sum_{k=0}{n}pk = 1\underbrace{000 \ldots 000}_{n+1}{}_{(p)}1+(p−1)k=0∑n​pk=1n+1000…000​​(p)​

And substituting the number in base ppp by its value in decimal:

1+(p−1)∑k=0npk=pn+1  ⟹  ∑k=0npk=pn+1−1p−11 + (p - 1)\sum_{k=0}{n}pk = p{n+1} \implies \sum_{k=0}{n}pk = \frac{p{n+1} - 1}{p - 1}1+(p−1)k=0∑n​pk=pn+1⟹k=0∑n​pk=p−1pn+1−1​
