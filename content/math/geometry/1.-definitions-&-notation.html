  1. Points are denoted by big letters, lines are denoted by small letters.
  2. Sentences: Point JJJ is on line kkk and line kkk passes through point JJJ, are equal in meaning. Symbolically they are denoted by J∈kJ \in kJ∈k.

  7. Circle C(O;r)\mathcal{C}(O; r)C(O;r) with center OOO and radius rrr is a set of all points of a plane, which distance from point OOO is equal to rrr.
  8. Disc D(O;r)\mathcal{D}(O; r)D(O;r) with center OOO and radius rrr is a set of all points of a plane, which distance from point OOO is less than or equal to rrr.
  9. Two rays with a common start and with one of two areas, which these rays divide the plane into, is an angle. Rays that form the angle are its arms, and their common start is the vertex of an angle. The area of a plane that is mentioned earlier is an inside. Angle is zero, when it has an empty inside, straight angle is an angle which arms are part of a single line, a complete angle is an angle which arms are equal.   16. Two angles with a single common arm, and other arms being complementary rays of the same line, are supplementary angles.
  17. A complete angle with both arms on a single line kkk is a half-plane with edge kkk.
